# Replit Polish Checklist

Use this to keep the frontend work disciplined while the permission plumbing is evaluated separately.

## Priority order

### 1. Activity
This is the hero surface.

Check:
- intent copy is stronger than raw transaction framing
- hierarchy between intent / status / amount is clear
- pending request card feels alive and premium
- trusted destination rail feels useful and trusted
- blocked states are human-readable and visually distinct
- spacing and row rhythm feel deliberate

### 2. Money
Check:
- language is rigorously non-custodial
- budget / allocation / spend hierarchy is clear
- connected wallet explanation is elegant
- actions do not imply app custody
- spend permissions are visible and understandable

### 3. Scout
Check:
- Scout feels like an operating state, not a profile stub
- spending source is clear
- verification is subtle but present
- active permissions and constraints are legible

### 4. Rules
Check:
- rules remain plain English
- toggles feel intentional, not default
- rule descriptions are readable and useful
- screen feels productized, not like settings boilerplate

## Red flags
- crypto dashboard feel
- raw hex in primary UI
- too much explanatory text
- generic fintech shell energy
- duplicate surfaces telling the same story
- custody ambiguity
- fake or overly literal vendor identities

## Product rules
- Base-first, Ethereum-native
- non-custodial
- activity feed is the product
- spend permissions are first-class
- trust is made visible through receipts and rules

## Do not do
- do not widen scope
- do not add new major surfaces casually
- do not re-architect nav again
- do not clutter the UI with protocol branding

## Goal
The frontend should feel inevitable, trustworthy, and specific — not just impressive for a generated prototype.
